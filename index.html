The résumé problem nobody talks about

🕳️

Memory decay

You can't accurately describe a job you held eight years ago. The specifics — the metrics, the stories, the wins — fade. By the time you need them, they're gone.

🔁

Constant rewriting

Every application means rewriting the same career history from scratch, reformatting for a new job description, and hoping you remembered everything important.

📉

Underselling yourself

The bullet points that make it onto a two-page résumé represent maybe 10% of what you've actually done. The rest disappears — and so does your negotiating leverage.

The solution

One archive. Every job. Updated as you go.

The Perpetual Résumé — the Perp — is a structured career archive you build once and update forever. It captures everything: responsibilities, achievements, and the STAR stories that prove what your bullet points claim. When you need a résumé, you pull from it. You never start from scratch again.

📋 Complete career history

Every role, going back to your first job. Dates, companies, titles, responsibilities — all in one place, all structured, all searchable.

⭐ STAR stories that stick

Situation, Task, Action, Result — the interview-ready narratives that back up every achievement. Written while the memory is fresh.

🔓 You own your data

Your Perp is a JSON file. Export it, fork it, back it up. No lock-in. No subscription required to access your own career history.

🤖 AI-ready structure (subscription)

The structured format was designed from the start to feed an AI tailoring engine — paste a job description, get a matched résumé in minutes.
